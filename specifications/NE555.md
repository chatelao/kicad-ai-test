## Astable Operation
- $T_{high} = 0.693 \cdot (R1 + R2) \cdot C$
- $T_{low} = 0.693 \cdot R2 \cdot C$
- $T = 0.693 \cdot (R1 + 2 \cdot R2) \cdot C$
- $f = 1.44 / ((R1 + 2 \cdot R2) \cdot C)$
- $Duty Cycle = (R1 + R2) / (R1 + 2 \cdot R2)$

For 1Hz frequency and 30% active (high) duty cycle:
To get < 50% duty cycle, we need a diode across R2.
With diode across R2:
- $T_{high} = 0.693 \cdot R1 \cdot C$
- $T_{low} = 0.693 \cdot R2 \cdot C$

Target: 1Hz, 30% High, 70% Low.
$T = 1s \implies T_{high} = 0.3s, T_{low} = 0.7s$.
$0.3 = 0.693 \cdot R1 \cdot C$
$0.7 = 0.693 \cdot R2 \cdot C$
$R1 / R2 = 3/7$
If $C = 10\mu F = 10 \cdot 10^{-6} F$:
$R1 = 0.3 / (0.693 \cdot 10^{-5}) \approx 43290 \Omega \approx 43k\Omega$
$R2 = 0.7 / (0.693 \cdot 10^{-5}) \approx 101010 \Omega \approx 100k\Omega$
